Welcome to Kashmir

Kashmir is a region known for its breathtaking landscapes, rich culture, and deep historical roots. For centuries, it has been a center of learning, spirituality, trade, and natural beauty.

Historical Background (Before 1947)

Before 1947, Jammu and Kashmir was a princely state during the period of British rule in the Indian subcontinent. It was governed by a local ruler (the Maharaja) and enjoyed internal autonomy, while external affairs were influenced by British paramountcy.

Jammu and Kashmir was not a part of British India, and it was not part of India or Pakistan before 1947. At the time of the partition of the subcontinent in August 1947, princely states were given the option to decide their future.

Culture & Heritage

Kashmir has a unique cultural identity shaped by centuries of Hindu, Buddhist, and Islamic influences. Its traditions, languages, music, handicrafts, and cuisine reflect this diverse heritage.
